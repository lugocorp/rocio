Rocio Treminio-Lopez

Rocio Treminio-Lopez was first sworn in as Mayor Pro-tem for the Town of Brentwood in November of 2014. May 5, 2015 she officially won her seat.

In 1999, after completing extensive minority training, Rocio began her career working with the Alice Hamilton Occupational Health Center. She also spent four years working in the financial sector for Bank of America, but she yearned to serve the community. In 2007 she began work on the Healthy Families Program for the Child Resource Center in Prince George’s Co. doing home visitation for low income families with teenage first time mothers. Healthy Families is a national, research-based, home visiting program focusing on positive parent- child interaction, healthy childhood development to enhance school readiness and the prevention of poor childhood outcomes including abuse and neglect.

Currently she is working with the Economic Development Corporation (EDC) as a Latino Business Liaison where she is responsible for building relationships with local Latino businesses in Prince George’s County. As the liaison, she builds awareness about EDC’s small businesses and jobs resources, develops contacts for businesses and organizations and builds partnerships within the Community specializing on Local Chambers of Commerce; Latino companies and faith based organizations.

Mayor Treminio–Lopez, married mother of four, grew up in El Salvador, and immigrated to the United States at the age of 14 and moved to the Washington, DC area in 1995. In 2020 Mayor Treminio-Lopez received her Bachelor in Human Resources Management from the University of Maryland Global College.
